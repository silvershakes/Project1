BJP wins 717 Gram Panchayats, NCP (Ajit) second with 407 Seats

Results of 2,226 out of the 2,359 gram panchayat results in Maharashtra on Monday showed that the BJP won in 717 gram panchayats, followed by the Ajit Pawar faction of the NCP with 407, Eknath Shinde-led Shiv Sena with 262 whereas the Congress has won 207 seats, NCP's Sharad Pawar faction got 185 and the Thackeray's Sena faction


The ruling BJP claimed victory in the gram panchayat elections — results of which were announced Monday — announcing that it had won 700 of the total 2,359 local governing bodies.

The Ajit Pawar-led NCP claimed that it came second by bagging more than 400 gram panchayats. While the ruling Shiv Sena lead by Chief Minister Eknath Shinde claimed the third spot with over 262 gram panchayats.

The elections for the 2,359 gram panchayats took place on Sunday, which recorded a 75 per cent voter turnout.

BJP chief spokesperson Keshav Upadhye said, “ The BJP won 778 grampanchayats, Shiv Sena (Shinde faction) 301, NCP (Ajit faction) 407. The total tally of the ruling combine is 1,486.”

Upadhye said that the Congress won 287 gram panchayats, NCP (Sharad Pawar faction) 144 and Shiv Sena (UBT) 115. While independents won 327 gram panchayats, results are still awaited for 39 in Gadchiroli.

Meanwhile, the Congress stated that the MVA had won 1,312 gram panchayats and dismissed the claims of ruling alliance stating that the gram panchayat elections are not contested on party symbols but panels.

maharashtra gram panchayat polls The elections for the 2,359 gram panchayats took place on Sunday, which recorded a 75 per cent voter turnout.

Gram panchayat elections in Maharashtra are not fought on election symbols, but leaders put up panels that contest the elections.

With elections being not fought on party symbols, it is difficult to ascertain the veracity of these claims.

This is the first gram panchayat elections held after the split in NCP on July 3.

The elections also took place amidst the Maratha reservation protests.

In a statement issued Monday, Deputy Chief Minister Devendra Fadnavis said, “The results show that the public mandate is for BJP, Shiv Sena (Shinde faction) and NCP (Ajit Pawar faction) which has been relentlessly pursuing the policies and programmes for welfare of people. The results are the mandate for the leadership and good governance of Prime Minister Narendra Modi.”

Reacting to the results, Chief Minister Eknath Shinde Monday said, “The gram panchayat elections are a befitting reply to the Opposition…They have shown the MVA their right place.”
